ANSWER KEY:
Big Question: How does a roller coaster turn height into speed without an engine?
Answer: A roller coaster converts potential energy (height) into kinetic energy (speed). The higher the first hill, the faster the coaster goes at the bottom â€” no engine needed!

Simulation Answers:
â€¢ First Drop Scenario: Starting at maximum height, PE is at its maximum and KE is near zero. As the coaster descends, PE decreases smoothly while KE increases by exactly the same amount. At the bottom, KE is maximized â€” this is where the coaster is fastest.
â€¢ Heavy vs. Light Coaster: Doubling the mass doubles BOTH PE at the top and KE at the bottom. However, since KE = Â½mvÂ², the speed at the bottom is the same! More mass means more energy, but the speed doesn't change â€” a key insight.

Reflection Exemplars:
â€¢ Q: Why must the first hill be tallest?
  A: The chain lift gives the coaster all of its energy at the start by raising it to maximum height. After that, the total energy can only decrease (due to friction). Since PE = mgh, the coaster can never reach a height higher than it started because it doesn't have enough energy.
â€¢ Q: Why does the coaster eventually stop?
  A: In the real world, friction and air resistance convert kinetic energy into heat. This 'lost' energy reduces the total available for PE-KE transformation. The energy isn't destroyed â€” it becomes thermal energy â€” but it's no longer useful for motion.
