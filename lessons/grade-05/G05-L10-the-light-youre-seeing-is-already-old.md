### Title
**The Light You're Seeing Is Already Old â€” When You Look at a Star, You're Seeing the Past**

### Overview
Students model the relationship between star distance, star luminosity, light travel time, and apparent brightness â€” discovering that the night sky is not a live picture of the universe but a collection of images from the past, some thousands or millions of years old. By building a cause-and-effect model showing how both distance and luminosity determine what we see from Earth, students directly engage with the 5-ESS1-1 standard in one of the most philosophically mind-bending lessons in the entire curriculum. The revelation that some visible stars may no longer exist creates authentic wonder.

### NGSS Standard
**5-ESS1-1**: Support an argument that differences in the apparent brightness of the sun compared to other stars is due to their relative distances from Earth.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain that a star's apparent brightness depends on both its distance and its actual luminosity
- Model how distance affects both the light travel time and the apparent brightness we observe
- Predict how a star's apparent brightness changes as distance increases
- Articulate the concept that observing stars means observing the past
