### Title
**Powered by the Sun â€” How Pizza, Basketball, and Your Heartbeat Are All Solar Energy**

### Overview
Students trace the energy chain from sunlight to photosynthesis to the food they eat and the activities they do. By modeling how sunlight intensity and temperature drive photosynthesis, which in turn determines how much energy is available to animals, students discover that virtually all life on Earth is running on stored solar power. This lesson creates a personal connection between the sun and students' own bodies â€” anchoring the energy transfer standard in something immediately relevant.

### NGSS Standard
**5-PS3-1**: Use models to describe that energy in animals' food (used for body repair, growth, motion, and to maintain body warmth) was once energy from the sun.

### Learning Objectives
- Trace the energy chain from the sun through plants to animals
- Model how sunlight intensity affects photosynthesis rate
- Explain how temperature influences plant energy production
- Predict what happens to animals when sunlight availability drops

### Component List (Starting Model: 4 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Sunlight Intensity | External | Amount of solar energy reaching plants (students can't control the sun) |
| Temperature | External | Ambient warmth that affects how efficiently plants photosynthesize |
| Photosynthesis Rate | Internal | How fast plants are converting sunlight to sugar/energy |
| Energy Available to Animals | Internal | How much food energy is in the ecosystem for consumers to use |
