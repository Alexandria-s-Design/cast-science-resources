### Title
**Where Does a Tree's Mass Come From? â€” A 1-Ton Tree Got Most of Its Weight From Air**

### Overview
Students model one of the most mind-blowing facts in science: a massive tree gets most of its mass from the air, not from the soil. By building a cause-and-effect model showing how CO2 from the atmosphere and sunlight drive photosynthesis, which in turn builds a tree's physical mass, students directly observe the 5-LS1-1 standard in action. The counter-intuitive phenomenon â€” air becoming solid wood â€” creates immediate wonder and drives the investigation.

### NGSS Standard
**5-LS1-1**: Support an argument that plants get the materials they need for growth chiefly from air and water.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain that plants get most of their mass from carbon dioxide in the air, not soil
- Model how CO2 and sunlight drive photosynthesis
- Trace how photosynthesis converts air into the physical mass of a tree
- Predict what happens to tree growth when CO2 or sunlight is removed
