### Overview
Students model the surprising distribution of Earth's water and the forces that control how much fresh water is actually available to drink. By building a cause-and-effect model of precipitation, temperature, freshwater availability, and evaporation, students discover why less than 1% of Earth's water is accessible â€” and how climate and geography determine which communities have enough. This lesson builds urgency around water scarcity while making the system immediately understandable.

### NGSS Standard

### Learning Objectives
- Describe the distribution of Earth's water across its major reservoirs
- Model how precipitation and temperature control freshwater availability
- Explain how evaporation reduces the amount of water accessible to communities
- Predict how changes in climate patterns affect freshwater supplies
